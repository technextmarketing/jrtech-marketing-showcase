05 · Buyer Behaviour

How B2B Buyers Find Kitchen Suppliers in 2025

The commercial kitchen procurement journey has shifted almost entirely online — buyers shortlist vendors before the first phone call is ever made.

1

Problem Recognition

New outlet opening, equipment failure, capacity expansion, or energy cost review triggers the search. Timeline: 1–2 weeks of internal discussion.

2

Online Research (Google + AI)

Most buyers begin with Google. Search terms like "commercial kitchen malaysia", "robot chef system", "industrial dishwasher supplier". A handful of vendors are shortlisted based on first-page results and website credibility.

3

Social Proof Validation

LinkedIn company page reviewed for recent posts, project photos, and follower count. Google Reviews checked — a weak rating eliminates many vendors at this stage before any contact is made.

4

Shortlist & RFQ

2–3 vendors contacted for quotation. Companies with clear product pages, case studies, and enterprise client lists are favoured. Average B2B kitchen deal: RM 120K–2M.

5

Decision & Approval

Final decision involves procurement, operations, and finance. Vendors with documented case studies (e.g., Intel fit-out, Gleneagles installation) dramatically increase conversion at this stage.
